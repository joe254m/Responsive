Audi A6

The 2020 Audi A6 Allroad is a new station-wagon model with standard all-wheel drive and body cladding to make it look more SUV-like. We took it to Beaver Island, a remote location in the middle of Lake Michigan, for some exploring.

BMW 7 SERIES

The 7-series' extensive list of luxury equipment includes leather or optional cashmere upholstery, an 18-speaker Bowers & Wilkins audio system (audiophiles can upgrade to a 36-speaker setup), and available electrically powered doors that close at the touch of a buttonâ€”like those on a Rolls-Royce.

Chevrolet Corvette ZR1

The 2026 Chevrolet Corvette C8 ZR1 has 1064 horsepower radiating from its mid-mounted twin-turbo V-8. The already proven C8 chassis gets tweaked for duty with a standard adaptive suspension and massive brakes.

Ferrari 12 Clindri

The 12Cilindri arrives for the 2025 model year with a naturally aspirated 6.5-liter V-12 engine that breathes deeply and revs to an ear-splitting 9500 rpm. The beastly engine that snarls under the long hood pumps out 819 horsepower and 500 pound-feet of torque, which is sent to the rear wheels via an eight-speed dual-clutch transmission.

Mazda CX-5

The current Mazda CX-5 crossover has been in production since 2017, an eternity in the car business. Even among newer compact-crossover rivals, though, the CX-5 continues to shine as one of the most attractive choices that's also one of the best to drive. Mazda debuted the slightly larger, slightly pricier CX-50 in 2023, but the CX-5 lineup was not scaled back to make way for its new stablemate.

Hyundai Elantra

The 2025 Hyundai Elantra wears a snazzy look and offers several high-end features that enhance its appeal. No one needs to know you spent less than $30,000. Three powertrains are offered, starting with a base four-cylinder with 147 horsepower.

Jeep Compass

The Compass was always the more attractive of the two, with greater interior space and tidier dimensions. The Compass is powered by a relatively efficient 200-hp four-cylinder backed by an unobtrusive eight-speed automatic, and while not as fun to drive as many of its competitors it does have polite on-road manners and a comfortable ride.

Porsche 718 Boxster

The soft-top Boxster shares powertrains and just about everything else but its roof with the hard-top Cayman coupe, and while you can get both with nearly 500 horsepower, even the 300-hp base models are soul-satisfying, as they benefit from the same fantastic transmissions and wonderfully tuned chassis as the more expensive, more powerful versions.

Volkswagen Golf GTI

The VW Golf GTI has combined the same desirable attributes for decades: sharp handling, an eager powertrain, and surprising practicality. The newest model doesn't diverge from that formula, but it does introduce some new technology while losing the option of a manual transmission.
